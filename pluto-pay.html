00 Project Overview

Design Challenge

Allow anyone to shop, transfer money, and more without a debit or credit card or the need to visit a physical bank or store.

The Goal

Design an e-wallet app that will provide users with the tools necessary to shop online or transfer money without the use of a debit or credit card.

My Role

My job is to take the project from the research stage to where the project will be handed off to the development team.

01 Competitive Analysis

Overview

In order to create a foundation for my design solution I needed educate myself on what solutions were already available to users. The best way to do this would be performing a competitive analysis on two top competitor apps: PayPal and Venmo.

By assessing the strengths and weaknesses of the two apps, I was able to set guidelines for my own solution. In addition, I identified key opportunities to win users over.

Opportunities

  • Usability
  • Security Risks
  • Technical Support
  • Need for "split bill" feature

05 Usability Testing

Test Overview

There were six participants for the usability tests. Tests consisted of scenarios where the participant would have to perform certain tasks utilizing each feature of the app.

Test Objectives

  1. Is there friction in the setup process for the Group Pay and Budget features?
  2. How long does it take each participant to perform the tasks?
  3. Does the app's navigation flow well?
  4. Is the user able to transfer money to contacts with ease?

Affinity Map

Like the interviews in the second phase, quotes, observations, and errors were categorized via an affinity map.

Errors

Takeaways

The tasks pertaining to the Onboarding process and Transfer features both scored high on the SEQ scale with a 6.67 out of 7. The tasks pertaining to the Group Pay and Budget featues scored lower with a 5.67 and 5.75 out of 7. Below are the key takeaways from the tests:

  • The onboarding and transfer features were frictionless
  • The recents tab on the contacts page confused participants
  • The Budget feature needs to be simplified and better serve users
  • Transactions lack a confirmation prompt in the flow

Revisions

With the above takeaways in mind, the next was to make these changes a reality before moving on. Errors would categorized by severity, which I then used to decide what changes were imperative and low priority. Below are just a few of the revisions made.

Revision #1

Budget Setup: Level 3

The budget setup did not flow properly for some users, as they were confused by the term “deadline” and not being able to select a start date. To combat this I have rearranged the setup include a start date option, as well as the ability to set the budget to repeat weekly, bi-weekly, monthly, etc. or not repeat at all.

Revision #2

Group Pay Setup: Level 2

It became quite apparent that the recents tab is not necessary when adding members to the group pay feature. Not only did it cause users to hesitate, but one of the participants thought “recent” referred to groups, not contacts. The solution on the right removes the recents tab, while still allowing users to find the contacts they are looking for.
